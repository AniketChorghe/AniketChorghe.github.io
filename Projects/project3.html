PROJECT 03

Hand Grinder DFMA Redesign

DFM DFA Cost Estimation Manufacturing Process
Why This Project?

In its conventional form, a hand grinder involves components and design features that are not optimized for cost-effective manufacturing or efficient assembly. Many consumer products contain parts that could be consolidated, simplified, or manufactured using more economical processes.

This project focused on applying Design for Manufacturing (DFM) and Design for Assembly (DFA) principles to the Makita 9557NB hand grinder, aiming to reduce part count, simplify joining methods, and minimize production costs while maintaining functionality and durability.

How Was It Done?

The project followed a comprehensive DFMA methodology:

  • Product Disassembly: Complete teardown and documentation of all 49 parts, analyzing each component's function, material, and manufacturing process.
  • DFM Analysis: Evaluated each part for manufacturing complexity, examining tolerances, geometry, material selection, and machining requirements.
  • DFA Analysis: Assessed assembly efficiency by analyzing handling time, insertion difficulty, and fastening methods for each component.
  • Cost Modeling: Developed detailed cost estimates for a production volume of 100,000 units per year, including material, processing, and labor costs.
  • Redesign Recommendations: Identified opportunities for part consolidation, material substitution, and process optimization.
