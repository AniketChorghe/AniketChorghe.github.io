PROJECT 05

Double Slope Solar Still — CFD Simulation

ANSYS Fluent VOF Multiphase CFD Heat Transfer
Why This Project?

Access to clean drinking water remains a critical challenge in many regions worldwide. Solar distillation offers a sustainable, low-cost solution for producing freshwater from saline or contaminated sources using only solar energy.

This project aimed to develop a comprehensive CFD model of a double slope solar still to understand the complex multiphase heat and mass transfer phenomena, predict freshwater yield, and optimize design parameters for improved efficiency.

How Was It Done?

The simulation was developed using ANSYS Fluent with the following approach:

  • Geometry & Meshing: Created a 3D model of the double slope solar still with appropriate boundary conditions for basin, glass cover, and insulated walls.
  • Multiphase Modeling: Implemented VOF (Volume of Fluid) method to track the water-air interface and capture evaporation/condensation phenomena.
  • Turbulence Model: Applied RNG k-ε turbulence model to accurately predict natural convection inside the still enclosure.
  • Solar Loading: Used solar ray tracing to simulate realistic solar irradiation from 9 AM to 4 PM with transient boundary conditions.
  • Heat Transfer: Coupled conduction, convection, and radiation heat transfer mechanisms for comprehensive thermal analysis.
