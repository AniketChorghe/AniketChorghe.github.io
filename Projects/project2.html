PROJECT 02

Fuselage Structural Design & Compression Test

NX Siemens FEA 3D Print Buckling Analysis
Why This Project?

Aircraft fuselage structures must withstand significant compressive loads during flight while maintaining minimal weight. Understanding buckling behavior is critical for aerospace structural design, as thin-walled structures are susceptible to instability under axial compression.

This project aimed to design, analyze, and physically validate a semi-monocoque fuselage structure with stiffeners, bridging the gap between theoretical FEA predictions and real-world structural performance through 3D printing and compression testing.

How Was It Done?

The project followed a systematic design-analysis-manufacturing workflow:

  • CAD Modeling: Designed a 34-inch semi-monocoque fuselage in NX Siemens with integrated hat-section stiffeners for buckling resistance.
  • Stiffener Selection: Evaluated T, Hat, and Z section geometries using analytical Euler buckling calculations to select optimal configuration.
  • FEA Analysis: Performed linear static and eigenvalue buckling analysis in ANSYS Workbench with mesh convergence studies.
  • 3D Printing: Fabricated the fuselage in six sections using Prusa MK4S with PLA filament, following specific print orientation strategies.
  • Compression Testing: Validated FEA predictions through physical compression tests with fixed bottom and pinned top boundary conditions.

The stiffened fuselage design achieved a critical buckling load of 1,626 lbs with over 50% stress reduction compared to the unstiffened configuration. FEA predictions showed excellent correlation with analytical calculations, demonstrating less than 2% deviation.
