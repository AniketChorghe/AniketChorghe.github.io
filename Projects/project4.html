PROJECT 04

Ceiling Fan Material & Process Selection

ANSYS Granta Material Selection Failure Analysis
Why This Project?

The Maverick Coastal 60-inch Outdoor Ceiling Fan experienced nine reported incidents of blade cracking or breaking during operation, posing significant impact injury hazards. Approximately 6,400 units were sold in the United States, leading to a product recall.

The primary failure mode was identified as fatigue from cyclic loading during continuous rotation. This project aimed to select optimal materials and manufacturing processes to eliminate the risk of blade failure, ensuring safety and reliability while maintaining cost efficiency.

How Was It Done?

The project followed Ashby's systematic material selection methodology:

  • Translation: Defined constraints (Young's modulus, yield strength, fracture toughness, service temperature) and objectives (maximize fracture resistance and fatigue strength).
  • Screening: Used ANSYS Granta EduPack to filter 1,000+ materials based on minimum property requirements.
  • Ranking: Applied material indices (K1c/ρ for fracture resistance, σe/ρ for centrifugal loading) to rank candidate materials.
  • Documentation: Evaluated shortlisted materials including aluminum alloys, composites, and wood for detailed comparison.
  • Process Selection: Identified cold closed-die forging as the optimal manufacturing process for the selected material.

Selected Aluminum 7475-T7351 as the optimal material, offering fracture toughness of 46.4 MPa·m^0.5 (vs 1.52 MPa·m^0.5 for original ASA plastic) and fatigue strength of 205 MPa. Cold closed-die forging was recommended for enhanced grain structure and dimensional accuracy.
